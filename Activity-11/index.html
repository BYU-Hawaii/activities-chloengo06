What is Lungs Cancer?

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that originates in the cells of the lungs, typically in the lining of the air passages. It is characterized by uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells, forming tumors that can interfere with normal lung function. There are two main types: non-small cell lung cancer and small cell lung cancer, each with distinct characteristics and treatment approaches. Symptoms of lung cancer may include persistent cough, chest pain, shortness of breath, and coughing up blood. Early detection through screening and prompt medical intervention are crucial for improving outcomes in lung cancer patients.

Lungs cancer and smoke

Lung cancer and smoking cigarettes are deeply intertwined, with smoking being the leading cause of this deadly disease. The carcinogenic substances present in cigarette smoke, such as tar and nicotine, directly damage the cells lining the lungs over time, increasing the risk of cancer development. Studies have consistently shown that smokers are far more likely to develop lung cancer compared to non-smokers, with the risk escalating with the duration and intensity of smoking.

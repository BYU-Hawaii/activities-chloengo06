Math Fun Facts

  • Did you know that a "googol" is a number with 100 zeros? It's even larger than the number of atoms in the observable universe!
  • The number "pi" (Ï€) is a mathematical constant that never ends or repeats. It's approximately 3.14159.
  • If you shuffle a deck of cards properly, there's a good chance that exact order of cards has never been seen before in the history of the universe!

General Knowledge Fun Facts

  • Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still edible!
  • Octopuses have three hearts! Two pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps it to the rest of the body.
  • The shortest war in history lasted only 38 minutes, between Britain and Zanzibar on August 27, 1896.

Geography Fun Facts

  • Canada has the longest coastline of any country in the world, stretching over 202,080 kilometers (125,570 miles).
  • There are more than 7,000 islands in the Philippines, but only about 2,000 of them are inhabited.
  • Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth, is growing taller by about 4 millimeters each year due to geological activity.

Music Fun Facts

  • The world's largest grand piano was built by a 15-year-old in New Zealand. It's 18 feet long and has 85 keys instead of the usual 88.
  • Beethoven continued to compose music even after he went completely deaf. His Ninth Symphony is one of his most famous works.
  • Listening to music can improve your mood and even help you concentrate better. Some studies show that classical music, in particular, can enhance brain power!
